Lesson 3: Using If-Else Statements to Make Decisions

Control your program's flow with conditional statements based on true or false evaluations.

Introduction

Decision-making is an essential part of programming, allowing you to control the flow of your application based on conditions. In C#, if-else statements give your code the ability to branch and perform different actions depending on whether a certain condition is true or false. This conditional logic is fundamental for creating dynamic programs that can adapt to different inputs and situations, improving the overall functionality and responsiveness of your software.
